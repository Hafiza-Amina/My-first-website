  2. Definition of Tajweed

    3. Tajweed is derived from the Arabic root jawada which means to make wellor to improve. It means rectify and upgrador make beauty in recitation.

    Technical Meaning

    • Correct and good pronunciation in recitation

    According to Hazrat Ali(RA)

    It is the correct pronunciation of letters and the recongnition of waqf(a place where to pause as to stop)


  3. Harakaat

    4. Most importent harakaat are:

    • Fatah

      • This looks like a line on top of the letter.

    • Kashrah

      • This looks like aslanted line at the bottom of a letters.

    • Dammah

      • This looks like a comma sign on top of the letters.

  4. Types of Letters

    5.

    • Maddah Letters

    Maddah letters are the vowels of the Arabic Language. These are three numbers described below;

    • Alif

      • When Alif is followed by Fatha it is Alif Maddah.

    • Wao

      • When Wao is followed by Dammah it is wao maddah.

    • Yaa

      • Whan Yaa is followed by kasra it is Yaa Maddah.

  5. Leen Letters

    6.

    • Leen Letters

      There are two leen letters.

    • Wao

    • Yaa

      • When these two are followed by Fatha these are called leen letters
      and will be pronounced softly.
